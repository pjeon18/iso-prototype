The ISO Design Document

its time for a real one-on-one.

Every screen, every flow, and every why. ISO is a dating app built on one enforced constraint — one conversation at a time — and every UI/UX decision below exists to make that constraint feel like a gift instead of a limitation: warm, unhurried, and human.

UI/UX · v1.0 · July 2026 · companion to the PRD, build spec & motion brief
01 · Design philosophy

Six ideas govern every screen

These aren't aesthetic preferences — each one is a product decision wearing a design coat. When a screen faces a trade-off, the pillar wins.

1

Constraint is the feature

One live conversation, enforced in the state layer, not by disabled buttons. The UI's job is to make the wall feel like a doorway: the queue CTA stays tappable during a chat and answers with a kind toast — the product explains itself instead of graying out.

2

Presence, not punishment

The reply timer is a soft amber bar labeled "presence, not pressure." At zero: nothing bad happens — a gentle "still there?" line. Only a second silence suggests "seems like a natural pause." No red, no countdown bomb, no penalty.

3

Loud thresholds, calm baseline

Exactly five moments get the color-wave (850ms bloom from your fingertip): entering the queue, a match, a mutual yes, "we met," and a clean close. Everything else is quiet springs. Loud only reads as important because the baseline is calm.

4

Slow arrivals, instant feedback

Screens settle in reading order over ~1s — like walking into a room, not loading a slide. But every press responds in <120ms. Pacing = getting to know someone; feedback = the app is alive under your thumb.

5

Rank experiences, never people

Reflection asks how the conversation felt, never scores the person. The trend is a floor-bounded curve that cannot crash. No leaderboard of humans exists anywhere — that would be the roster with numbers.

6

A journal, never an inbox

Three tabs, forever: Queue · Chat · Profile. Memories is read-only with no reply affordance and no unread counts. Revival is blind and single-slot. Nothing accumulates, nothing rots, nothing begs.

02 · The system in one glance

Warm cream, one confident orange, motion as vocabulary

A single accent marks the primary/live element so hierarchy is never ambiguous. Green is reserved for positive presence ("here now", "real", mutual). Motion comes from named presets only — consistency is what separates crafted from generated.

08 · The navigation model

One surface you move around — not a stack of pages

Spatial continuity is what makes the app feel like one physical object.

The swipeable track

Queue · Chat · Profile live on a horizontal track you can drag between — content follows the finger, settles with momentum, and the tab indicator slides continuously with the drag. Interruptible mid-flight. The one-chat rule lives in content, never in navigation: the Chat tab is always reachable, showing your one connection or a breathing, designed empty state ("your one conversation will live here").

Direction encodes hierarchy

Deeper screens (settings, memories) slide in from the right over a gently parallaxing track; onboarding slides forward-left; full-screen takeovers (searching, match, live room, revival) own the whole viewport because in those moments the product wants all of you. Back always returns the way you came.

Why waves instead of page transitions at thresholds?

A route change says "different page." A color flood blooming from your fingertip says "you did something that matters." The five wave moments are the five sentences of the product thesis — commit, meet, choose each other, meet for real, end well — so they get a physical, tactile signature no ordinary navigation shares. The wave hands off (destination mounted beneath, 850ms in / 650ms clear) so the ceremony never costs a hard cut.

Accessibility & restraint

prefers-reduced-motion converts waves to brief tints and slides to fades — same order, same meaning, no vestibular cost. And everything staged is skippable by being fast underneath: no animation ever gates input on the happy path; splash buttons are the single deliberate exception (inert only while invisible).

09 · How the UI serves the product

The seams this design removes

Anxiety at the decision

Simultaneous sealed reveal + kind "no" copy means neither person ever answers second or gets rejected in public. The scariest moment in the loop is the most protected.

Shame in the metrics

Floor-bounded trend, private reflections, outcome answers nobody else sees. Numbers exist for the user's growth and the company's North Star — never for judging humans.

Friction at re-entry

Every ending — decline, close, block, no-match — lands on a warm screen with one-tap re-queue and a sanctioned exit ("take a break"). No dead ends anywhere in the graph.

Distrust of the business

The paywall pledges no pay-to-win; settings brag about what's missing; permissions explain themselves. The monetization is auditable from inside the UI.

Overwhelm at first touch

Staged splash, one-question onboarding, single-CTA home. At no point does a new user face more than one decision per screen.

The roster reflex

Three tabs, read-only memories, one revival slot, blind flags, silent decay. Every surface that could quietly become a list of people was designed so it structurally can't.
